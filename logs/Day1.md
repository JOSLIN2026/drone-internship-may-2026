# Drone Internship Log - Day 1

## Overview
Today was Day 1 of my Drone Technology Internship at Government Engineering College, Thrissur. I focused on the fundamental history, applications, and core classification of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs).

---

## 1. Introduction to UAVs
* **Definition:** UAV stands for Unmanned Aerial Vehicle.
* **History:** Explored the evolution of drones, including early military reconnaissance uses during the Vietnam War.
* **Modern Drivers:** Learned how modern drone technology evolved rapidly due to the commercial availability of:
  * Brushless motors
  * LiPo (Lithium Polymer) batteries
  * Miniaturized MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) sensors

---

## 2. Drone Types & Classifications
I learned about the distinct structural differences and use cases for various drone platforms:
* **Multirotor Drones:** Highly maneuverable, uses multiple rotors for lift (e.g., quadcopters).
* **Fixed-Wing Drones:** Resembles traditional airplanes; efficient for long-distance flights but requires runways or launchers.
* **VTOL (Vertical Take-Off and Landing):** Combines the efficiency of fixed-wing flight with the vertical takeoff capabilities of multirotors.
* **FPV (First Person View) Drones:** Controlled via a camera feed broadcasted directly to pilot goggles for precise, immersive flying.
