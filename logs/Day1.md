# Drone Internship Documentation – Day 1 Summary

I recently attended a **Drone Technology Internship** at Government Engineering College Thrissur focused on the fundamentals of UAVs (Unmanned Aerial Vehicles), drone architecture, and flight systems.

During the session, I explored how modern drones integrate electronics, embedded systems, aerodynamics, sensors, and control systems into a single intelligent platform.

---

## Topics Covered

### Introduction to UAVs
* Meaning of UAV – *Unmanned Aerial Vehicle*
* History and applications of drones, including reconnaissance during the Vietnam War
* Evolution of drone technology using:
  * Brushless motors
  * LiPo batteries
  * Miniaturized MEMS sensors

### Drone Types
* Multirotor drones
* Fixed-wing drones
* VTOL (Vertical Take-Off and Landing) systems
* FPV (First Person View) drones

### Drone Architecture

#### Frame / Skeleton
* Structure made using:
  * Carbon fiber
  * Plastic
  * Metal
* Importance of high strength-to-weight ratio

#### Propulsion System
* Propellers generate lift by pushing air
* Quadcopters use:
  * 2 clockwise (CW) motors
  * 2 counter-clockwise (CCW) motors
* Understanding pitch and stability

### Motors and ESC
* Difference between brushed and brushless motors
* Role of ESC (Electronic Speed Controller)
* ESC converts control signals into motor-driving power
* Understanding 3-phase motor operation

### Flight Controller – The Brain
* Role of MCU and MPU
* Flight controller responsibilities:
  * Reading sensor data
  * Processing pilot input
  * Stabilizing the drone
  * Controlling motor speed

### Sensors and Navigation
* Gyroscope
* Accelerometer
* GPS
* Barometer
* IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit)

### Signals and Control Systems
* PWM (Pulse Width Modulation)
* Duty cycle concepts:
  * 0%
  * 25%
  * 50%
  * 75%
  * 100%
* Average output voltage and motor control

### Batteries
* Lithium Polymer (LiPo) batteries
* Lithium-Ion batteries
* High energy density and power delivery characteristics

---

## Key Learnings
* Drones are highly multidisciplinary systems combining:
  * Embedded electronics
  * Aerodynamics
  * Control systems
  * Sensors
  * Communication systems
* Understanding how sensor feedback and PWM-based motor control stabilize a drone in flight
* Importance of documentation through blogs, videos, photos, and code sharing

---

## Reflection
This internship gave me a strong foundation in drone systems and helped me better understand how real-world UAV platforms are designed and controlled. It also strengthened my interest in embedded systems, robotics, and aerospace-related technologies.
